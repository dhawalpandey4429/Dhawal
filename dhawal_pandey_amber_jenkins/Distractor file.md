\#\# Section 6 — Distractor File Notes

\#\#\# Distractor Design Objective

Distractor artifacts should appear plausible within the household-records environment while contributing no evidence toward payment-status determination, obligation reconciliation, or discrepancy resolution.

\---

\#\#\# Allowed Distractor Themes

\- Community events  
\- School activities  
\- Travel planning  
\- Office environments  
\- General household lifestyle content  
\- Membership confirmations  
\- Event registrations

\---

\#\#\# Prohibited Distractor Content

Distractor files must not contain:

\- Invoice identifiers  
\- Payment identifiers  
\- Transaction identifiers  
\- Account numbers  
\- Billing amounts  
\- Outstanding balances  
\- Payment confirmations  
\- Service-provider records  
\- Utility-service references  
\- Insurance-payment references

\---

\#\#\# Noise Artifact Requirements

Each distractor artifact must:

\- Appear realistic.  
\- Be internally consistent.  
\- Remain unrelated to household bill reconciliation.  
\- Contain no evidence supporting GTFA findings.  
\- Contain no evidence contradicting GTFA findings.

\---

\#\#\# Decoy Image Guidance

Decoy images may include:

\- Office workspaces  
\- Meeting rooms  
\- Building interiors  
\- Community activities

Decoy images must not display:

\- Bills  
\- Receipts  
\- Financial statements  
\- Payment screens  
\- Transaction details

\---

\#\#\# Decoy Document Guidance

Examples:

\- Community fair flyer  
\- School activity schedule  
\- Family trip itinerary

These documents may contain dates and names but must not contain financial evidence relevant to the task.
